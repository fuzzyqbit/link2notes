Link To Notes

Drop an audio file, get beginner-friendly sheet music.

How to use

  1. Copy the YouTube link of the song you want.
  2. Open cobalt.tools, paste the link, choose Audio, and download the MP3.
  3. Drop the MP3 into the Audio file box below.
  4. Pick your instrument, then press Convert.

Important: only solo instrument tracks work well. One instrument playing one note at a time. No chords, no backing tracks, no singing over the music, no full bands. Pick a video that's just a single flute, violin, piano melody line, etc.

Why a separate site for the download?

Browsers can't pull audio directly from YouTube — only YouTube's own player can. cobalt.tools is a free, ad-free converter that runs in your browser, returns the MP3, and forgets your link afterwards. No account needed.

Other options that work: any tool that gives you an MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, or FLAC file. If you already have a recording, skip step 2 and drop the file straight in.
