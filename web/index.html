Link To Notes

Drop an audio file, get beginner-friendly sheet music.

How to use

  1. Open the YouTube video of the song you want.
  2. Record it with your Chromebook's built-in screen recorder (see below).
  3. Drop the recording into the Audio file box below.
  4. Pick your instrument, then press Convert.

Important: only solo instrument recordings work. One instrument playing one note at a time. No chords, no backing tracks, no singing over the music, no full bands. Pick a video that's just a single flute, violin, piano melody line, etc.

How to record a YouTube video on a Chromebook
  1. Open the YouTube video but don't press play yet.
  2. Press Shift + Ctrl + Show Windows (the key with rectangles, usually F5).
  3. In the toolbar at the bottom of the screen, click the video camera icon (screen record).
  4. Click the gear / settings icon and turn on Record microphone — or better, hold your Chromebook close to the speakers so the mic picks up the audio cleanly.
  5. Choose Record partial screen and drag a box over the YouTube player (or pick full screen).
  6. Click Record, then press play on the video. Let the whole solo play through.
  7. When done, click the red stop button in the shelf (bottom-right corner of screen).
  8. The file saves to your Downloads folder as a .webm video — drop that into the box below.

Tip: quiet the room before recording. Background noise, voices, or other instruments will confuse the converter.
